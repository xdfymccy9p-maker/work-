Equal Pay

Why women still earn less in similar jobs.

Equal pay means that men and women doing the same work should receive the same salary. However, pay gaps continue to appear in workplaces due to hiring bias, negotiation patterns, and limited salary transparency practices.

References
  • CIPD. (2023). Gender pay gap reporting.
  • Hegewisch & Tesfaselassie. (2022). Equal pay policies.

Remote Work

Flexibility vs. pressure.

Remote work offers flexibility yet increases pressure for many women who experience an overlap between job duties and household responsibilities.

References
  • Alfarran. (2021). Remote work and women's balance.
  • Bulińska-Stangrecka. (2020). Work-life conflict in remote work.

Women in Leadership

Representation at the top remains limited.

Women remain underrepresented in leadership roles. Research shows companies benefit from diverse management and experience smaller gender gaps when women participate in decision-making.

References
  • OECD. (2025). Women in leadership.
  • Magda et al. (2019). Women managers and pay gaps.

Overtime Culture

Long hours = serious health risks.

Working beyond 55 hours weekly significantly increases health risks. Women face additional stress due to domestic unpaid work, leading to burnout and reduced well-being.

References
  • WHO & ILO. (2021). Long working hours.
  • Yang et al. (2023). Overtime and well-being.

The Gender Pay Gap

A wider economic pattern.

The gender pay gap reflects structural differences in job sectors, access to leadership, and social expectations. EU reports show women earning around 12% less on average.

References
  • European Parliament. (2025). Gender pay gap report.
  • Khan. (2025). Women’s economic empowerment.
